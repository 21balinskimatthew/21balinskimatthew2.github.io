Mike Tyson

"The Baddest Man on the Planet"

Here's a time line of Mike Tyson's life:

  • 1966 - Born in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1979 - A boxing instructor at a New York State correction facility for boys brought Tyson to the attention of Cus D'Amato, who had guided Floyd Patterson to the heavyweight title.
  • 1985 - In his professional debut, Tyson defeats Hector Mercedes in one round.
  • 1986 - Tyson knocks out Trevor Berbick in the second round, winning the WBC heavyweight title to become the youngest heavyweight champion in history at age 20
  • 1987 - Tyson defeats James "Bonecrusher" Smith at Las Vegas to win the WBA heavyweight title. Tyson knocks out Pinklon Thomas in the sixth round at Las Vegas to retain his WBA-WBC heavyweight titles. Knocks out Tyrell Biggs in the seventh round in Atlantic City to retain the world heavyweight title.
  • 1988 - Tyson knocks out Larry Holmes in the fourth round to retain the world heavyweight title. Actress Robin Givens and Tyson are married in New York. Tyson knocks out Tony Tubbs to retain the world heavyweight title. Tyson sues manager Bill Cayton to break their contract, then knocks out Michael Spinks in 91 seconds to retain the world heavyweight title. Tyson is knocked unconscious after driving his BMW into a tree. Three days later, the New York Daily News reports the accident was a "suicide attempt" caused by a "chemical imbalance" that made him violent and irrational.
  • 1989 - Tyson knocks out Frank Bruno to retain the world heavyweight title. Tyson knocks out Carl "The Truth" Williams to retain the world heavyweight title.
  • 1990 - In a stunning upset, Tyson is knocked out by James "Buster" Douglas in the 10th round and loses his world heavyweight title. A New York City civil jury finds Tyson committed battery in the Sandra Miller case, but Miller is awarded just $100 in damages because the jury decides Tyson's behavior was "not outrageous."
  • 1991 - In what would be his last fight before his legal problems, Tyson defeats Razor Ruddock in 12 rounds.
  • 1995 - Tyson is released from the Indiana Youth Center near Plainfield, Ind. Begins comeback with 89 seconds victory over Peter McNeeley in Las Vegas. Knocks out Buster Mathis, Jr. in third round in Philadelphia.
  • 1996 - Knocks out Frank Bruno in third round to win the WBC heavyweight title in Las Vegas. Loses to Evander Holyfield when referee Mitch Halpern stops the bout in the 11th round.
  • 1997 - Tyson is disqualified after the third round of his rematch with Holyfield after he bites Holyfield twice, once on each ear. Tyson claims he was retaliating for a head butt inflicted by Holyfield that opened up a gash above his right eye. Referee Mills Lane ruled the butt was accidental. The Nevada State Athletic Commission, in a unanimous voice vote, revoked Mike Tyson's boxing license and fined him $3 million for biting Holyfield.
  • 1998 - Filed a $100 million lawsuit in U.S. District Court in New York against Don King, accusing the promoter of cheating him out of tens of millions of dollars. Sherry Cole and Chevelle Butts filed a $22 million lawsuit against Tyson claiming he verbally and physically abused them March 1 at a Washington bistro them at a restaurant after his sexual advances toward one of them were spurned.
  • 1999 - Tyson knocked out Francois Botha in the fifth round. Tyson admitted to trying to break Botha's arm during the fight. vs. Orlin Norris Tyson hit Norris after the bell in the 1st round and the fight was declared a no contest.
  • 2000 - vs. Lou Savarese. Tyson knocked the referee down in order to keep punching Savarese after the bout was stopped. Tyson was fined $187,500 for his behavior after his 38-second victory over Lou Savarese but escaped a ban from fighting again in Britain.
  • 2002 - Tyson and Lennox Lewis come to blows during their scheduled TV press conference hyping their April 6th meeting. It was alleged that Tyson bit Lennox's leg during the melee. In a 4-1 vote, the Nevada Athletic Commission on Tuesday rejected Tyson's request to get his Nevada boxing license back.
